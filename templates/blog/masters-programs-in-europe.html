To 10 Masters Programs in Europe for American Students

  • August 4, 2024
If you are American or use American English as your first language and are looking for an American-style university, there are Masters Programs in Europe that can be a game-changer for you! This Abroadin blog post includes a list of the best programs across prestigious universities, offering diverse fields from Business to Law. Let’s discover the ideal Masters course for you in Europe. 1. Executive Master’s in Leadership MSc, Masters Programs in Europe

  • University: City, University of London, Bayes Business School
  • Location: London, United Kingdom
  • QS World University Ranking: 352
  • Subject: Business and Management Studies
  • Duration: 24 Months
  • Official Website

The Executive Master’s in Leadership at City, University of London’s Bayes Business School is a 2-year part-time program, exclusively available through apprenticeship levy funding in England.

This program is for aspiring and current leaders with a solid academic background and 3-5 years of senior work experience. It aims to enhance managerial competencies across various industries.

The course structure, consisting of both classroom and work-based learning, provides a balanced mix of theoretical knowledge and practical application.

With 180 UK credits and 90 ECTS, the program’s total cost of almost USD 18,000 can be fully covered by eligible organizations’ apprenticeship levy funding.

These Masters programs in Europe include comprehensive modules such as Leading People and Change, Personal Leadership Development, and Managerial Economics, spread across six terms.

It culminates in a Work-Based Leadership Impact Project, which integrates learning with real-world business issues. Graduates will earn a Master’s in Science degree, a Senior Leader Level 7 Apprenticeship award, and a Chartered Management Institute membership.

 

2. History: Medieval and Renaissance Studies MA

  • University: University of Liverpool
  • Location: Liverpool, United Kingdom
  • QS World University Ranking: 165
  • Subject: History
  • Duration: 12 Months
  • Official Website

The Medieval and Renaissance Studies MA at the University of Liverpool offers the rich cultural tapestry of medieval and early modern periods.

This multidisciplinary program uses the university’s extensive expertise in history, literature, and languages to explore the art, philosophy, religion, politics, and societal structures of these eras.

Students analyze the evolution of these concepts and their lasting impact on modern society. Through advanced research skills, primary source analysis, and training in languages and paleography, students gain a profound understanding of these historical periods.

Students explore the exercise of secular and spiritual authority, the development of gender conceptions, and the construction of identities. Participation in Liverpool’s Centre for Medieval and Renaissance Studies provides an active research network and access to specialized resources.

This program is for both recent graduates and those returning to academia. It is ideal for individuals with a keen interest in religion, gender, and culture of the Renaissance and early modern eras.

By the end of the course, students will have completed a final dissertation or project, which displays their accumulated knowledge and research skills, preparing them for various professional and academic pathways.

3. Master’s Degree in Continuing Education in Medical Education

  • University: Universität Heidelberg
  • Location: Heidelberg, Germany
  • QS World University Ranking: 84
  • Subject: Medicine Related Studies
  • Duration: 24 Months
  • Official Website

The Master’s Degree in Medical Education (MME) at Universität Heidelberg is for improving the professionalism and quality of medical teaching of American students.

It aims at individuals responsible for planning and organizing medical degree programs. The course covers a broad range of topics including curriculum development, communication, teaching methodologies, educational research, leadership, and evaluation of medical facilities.

Graduates will be equipped to introduce innovative educational approaches within their institutions, significantly improving the quality of medical education.

This program is structured to be completed over four semesters, starting in the winter. Instruction is delivered in both German and English, with fees amounting to almost USD 8,400 per semester.

The course consists of eight in-person modules, each requiring attendance for five days (Monday to Friday) and spanning 18 months. After completing the modules, students undertake a Master’s thesis, which involves conducting an educational research project under expert supervision.

The modules are hosted by various universities in German-speaking countries, with the final module taking place at a different university each year, either in Germany or abroad.

This degree program not only aims to professionalize medical education but also promotes high-quality educational research and fosters cross-Germany exchange among medical faculty leaders.

 

4. American Studies (MA)

  • University: University of Szeged
  • Location: Szeged, Hungary
  • QS World University Ranking: 570
  • Subject: Sociology
  • Duration: 24 Months
  • Official Website

The American Studies MA program at the University of Szeged offers an in-depth exploration of US society, history, cultures, and literature. Initially, students engage with introductory courses covering American literature, history, and culture.

The curriculum emphasizes critical analysis, theoretical problems, and research direction, providing a comprehensive understanding of American history and societal dynamics.

Throughout these Masters programs in Europe, students improve their research and publication skills. Research areas include intellectual and cultural history, visual culture, and film studies.

The program, spanning four semesters, awards 120 credits and requires a BA diploma and advanced English proficiency for admission. The tuition fee is almost USD 2100 per semester.

Graduates often pursue careers in cultural and public administration, tourism, international business, teaching, translation, journalism, academia, editing, diplomacy, international relations, media, project management, or local government.

5. Financial Economics, Masters Programs in Europe

  • University: Maastricht University
  • Location: Maastricht, Netherlands
  • QS World University Ranking: 230
  • Subject: Economics and Econometrics
  • Duration: 12 Months
  • Official Website

The Master’s in Financial Economics at the School of Business and Economics is tailored for those passionate about financial markets and the evolving role of financial institutions.

Whether you’re interested in stock markets, derivatives, central banks, or innovative business models for the financial sector, this program offers a unique multidisciplinary approach combining finance, general economics, and quantitative techniques.

The program features three specializations: Asset Pricing, Banking, and Financial Analysis. With a learning model based on Problem-Based Learning, guest lectures, presentations, assignments, and papers, students gain practical and theoretical knowledge.

The one-year, full-time course starts in September and February and involves 8 hours of classes, 12 hours of group work, and 20 hours of independent study per week.

Graduates earn an MSc in Financial Economics, preparing them for careers in financial markets and institutions.

 

6. MA Gender Studies (Middle East and Islamic World)

  • University: University of Exeter
  • Location: Exeter, United Kingdom
  • QS World University Ranking: 169
  • Subject: Area Studies
  • Duration: 12 Months
  • Official Website

The MA in Gender Studies (Middle East and Islamic World) at the University of Exeter offers an advanced and critical study of gender theory and politics in the Middle East and Islamicate world.

This one-year full-time or two-year part-time program, based at the Streatham Campus, is designed to connect academic language with lived experiences.

The flexible curriculum allows students to tailor their studies to their particular interests, engaging in independent research using unique collections and archival resources.

Graduates of this program gain in-depth knowledge of gender issues in the region and develop expertise valued by both public and private sector employers.

The program emphasizes innovative teaching that connects gender studies with creativity and activism. Entry requirements include a 2:2 Honours degree, with consideration for applicants from diverse academic backgrounds or relevant work experience.

International students must meet specific English language requirements. Tuition fees for the 2024/25 entry are almost USD 31,400 per year for international students, with various scholarships available.

7. Microbiology and Microbial Biotechnology

  • University: University of Helsinki
  • Location: Helsinki, Finland
  • QS World University Ranking: 117
  • Subject: Biological Sciences
  • Duration: 24 Months
  • Official Website

The Master’s Program in Microbiology and Microbial Biotechnology (MMB) explores the role of microbes in life and the environment, equipping students with the expertise to tackle global challenges and innovate in biotechnological applications.

Students study fundamental questions about microbes’ roles in medicine, industry, and food production over two years.

The program emphasizes the development of practical skills and research techniques essential for studying viruses, bacteria, and other microorganisms, preparing graduates for careers in various sectors, including the food and drug industries.

These Masters programs in Europe offer 120 ECTS credits and are available in Finnish, English, and Swedish. For non-EU/EEA citizens, the annual tuition fee is almost USD 20,000.

 

8. Sports Physiotherapy MSc

  • University: University of Bath
  • Location: Bath, United Kingdom
  • QS World University Ranking: 150
  • Subject: Anatomy and Physiology
  • Duration: 36 Months
  • Official Website

The MSc in Sports Physiotherapy at the University of Bath is a specialized, part-time, distance learning program tailored for practicing physiotherapists seeking advanced expertise in sports physiotherapy.

This 3-year course combines flexible online learning with practical residential weeks. The curriculum covers advanced sports physiotherapy, athlete management, and performance enhancement, supported by an experienced team with a background in high-profile sporting events like the Commonwealth Games and the FIFA World Cup.

Students benefit from interactive online modules and residential teaching weeks, which include practical and clinical training. The program’s structure allows for concurrent practice in the field, making it ideal for working professionals.

Graduates will be equipped to apply evidence-based practices and innovate within the sports physiotherapy field.

 

9. Socio-legal Studies – LLM, Masters Programs in Europe

  • University: University of Kent
  • Location: Canterbury, United Kingdom
  • QS World University Ranking: 380
  • Subject: Law and Legal Studies
  • Duration: 12 Months
  • Official Website

Kent Law School offers advanced study opportunities in Socio-legal Studies through its Postgraduate LLM and PhD programs.

The LLM can be completed in one year full-time or two years part-time, while the PhD program spans three to four years full-time.

The research environment at Kent Law School is renowned for its vibrancy and support, benefiting from high rankings in the Research Excellence Framework (REF).

Students can choose between full-time and part-time study modes, with the latter requiring at least two days a week dedicated to research. For those unable to relocate, there is also an external research route.

 

10. Master of Cybersecurity

  • University: KU Leuven
  • Location: Leuven, Belgium
  • QS World University Ranking: 63
  • Subject: Cybersecurity
  • Duration: 12 Months
  • Official Website

The Master of Cybersecurity at KU Leuven offers a comprehensive curriculum designed to equip you with essential skills in cryptographic techniques, hardware and software security, and privacy.

These Masters programs in Europe not only improve your technical capabilities but also provide valuable insights into the legal, business, and operational frameworks of cybersecurity.

The 60-ECTS Master of Cybersecurity is a rigorous, one-year full-time program, taught in English, and is a collaborative effort between KU Leuven’s COSIC and DistriNet research units.

The course structure includes an introduction to key cybersecurity domains, followed by in-depth specialization in selected themes. Students will engage in research seminars and complete a master’s thesis, preparing them to tackle complex security challenges in various professional contexts.

This program is ideal for graduates in electrical engineering, computer science, or mathematics seeking to advance their expertise in the rapidly evolving field of cybersecurity.
